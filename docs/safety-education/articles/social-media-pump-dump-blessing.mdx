## Where the Scammer Won

<Warning>
- **Fake influencer authority:** The accounts looked credible, had followers, and seemed knowledgeable.
- **Coordination:** Multiple "influencers" amplified the same message, creating the illusion of consensus.
- **FOMO:** The rapid price action and hype made Blessing feel like she was missing out if she didn't buy.
- **Perfectly timed posts:** Each post came at the right moment to keep the hype alive and push more retail in.
</Warning>

## What Blessing Could Have Done (Prevention)

### Red Flags to Watch

<Check>**Influencer timing:** If an influencer suddenly shills a token hard for a few days, then goes quiet while the price crashes, assume they were paid or complicit.</Check>

<Check>**Profile verification:** Verify that influencers are real by: Checking their account age (new accounts = red flag).</Check>
<Check>**Profile verification:** Verify that influencers are real by: Reverse-image-searching their profile photo.</Check>

<Check>**On-chain analysis:** Check if the influencer's claimed holdings are real. If they post a screenshot of their portfolio, verify it on-chain.</Check>

<Check>**Coordinated shilling:** If multiple "influencers" are posting about a token at the same time, assume it's coordinated.</Check>

### Safe Behavior

<Check>**Distrust influencer calls.** Even if an influencer seems legitimate, assume they are either: Paid to shill by the project (common).</Check>
<Check>**Distrust influencer calls.** Even if an influencer seems legitimate, assume they are either: Exiting their own positions while encouraging others to buy.</Check>

<Check>**Do your own research.** Don't buy because someone with followers told you to.</Check>

<Check>**Exit when the shill stops.** If an influencer suddenly goes quiet about a token they were hyping, that's your signal to exit.</Check>

## Red Flags Checklist

<Warning>
- [ ] **Influencer suddenly shills a new token heavily.**
- [ ] **Multiple "influencers" all shill the same token around the same time.**
- [ ] **Influencer's profile is new or uses stolen photos.**
- [ ] **Price spikes during the shill period, then crashes when they stop posting.**
- [ ] **Project's announced "partnerships" are never confirmed by the partner.**
- [ ] **Influencer's claimed holdings cannot be verified on-chain.**
</Warning>

## How Pump-and-Dumps Work

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Step 1: Accumulation" icon="arrow-down">
**What happens:** Creators and insiders quietly accumulate tokens cheaply.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Step 2: Hiring Shillers" icon="users">
**What happens:** The creators pay fake influencers to hype the token.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Step 3: Hype Phase" icon="megaphone">
**What happens:** Multiple "influencers" shill the token on Twitter/TikTok/Instagram.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Step 4: Retail FOMO" icon="users">
**What happens:** New buyers flood in, chasing FOMO and hype.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Step 5: Coordination Exit" icon="arrow-up">
**What happens:** All shillers and creators dump simultaneously while retail is still buying.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Step 6: Collapse" icon="trending-down">
**What happens:** Price crashes 99%; shillers disappear; project goes quiet.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Action Steps: Protect Yourself

### Prevention

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Verify Influencer Credibility" icon="user-check">
**Check:**
- Account age and posting history
- Reverse image search profile photos
- On-chain verification of claimed holdings
- Whether they shill multiple tokens or have varied content
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Be Skeptical of Coordinated Shilling" icon="users">
**Rule:** If multiple influencers post about the same token simultaneously, assume it's coordinated and avoid.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do Your Own Research" icon="magnifying-glass">
**Rule:** Never buy based solely on influencer recommendations. Always verify independently.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Exit When Shill Stops" icon="log-out">
**Rule:** If an influencer suddenly goes quiet about a token they were hyping, that's your exit signal.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### If You're Already Involved

<Steps>
  <Step title="Monitor Influencer Activity">
    If they stop posting about the token, exit immediately.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Check On-Chain Activity">
    Verify if large wallets are selling. If so, exit.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Don't Hold Hoping for Recovery">
    Pump-and-dumps rarely recover. Take losses and move on.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Report Fake Influencers">
    Report to platforms (Twitter, Instagram) and warn others.
  </Step>
</Steps>
