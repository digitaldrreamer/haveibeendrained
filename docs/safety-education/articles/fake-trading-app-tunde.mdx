## Meet Tunde

Tunde is a 32-year-old freelancer who made ₦5 million over two years doing software development for startups. He wants to grow this capital passively. He's heard about crypto and trading but doesn't have time to day-trade himself.

One day, scrolling Twitter, he sees an ad: **"TradeSmart – AI-Powered Trading. 2.5% Daily Returns. Register Free."**

The landing page looks professional. Green-themed, testimonials from "successful traders," a 4.9-star rating (from 2,847 reviews), and a clear call-to-action: "Start Trading in 60 Seconds."

He signs up.

## What Happened

### Step 1: Onboarding

The app (available on Google Play, he confirmed) was slick. KYC was minimal—just email, phone, and a selfie. No ID check, no address verification.

### Step 2: First Deposit

Tunde added ₦500,000 via bank transfer to a local account. Within an hour, the app showed the credit.

### Step 3: Fake Trading & Profits

The app displayed a "portfolio" with BTC, ETH, and some altcoins. The dashboard showed a trading bot running automatically, generating orders every few minutes. Daily returns: 2.5% like clockwork.

After two weeks, Tunde's ₦500,000 had become ₦575,000 (+15% total). The profit felt real because the bot showed order history, though no external exchange could verify the trades.

### Step 4: Deposit More

Convinced, Tunde added ₦1 million more. Now his balance showed ₦1.575 million.

The daily returns continued. After 30 days, the app showed ₦1.8 million.

### Step 5: Attempt Withdrawal

Tunde decided to withdraw ₦500,000 and keep the "profits" in the app.

He clicked "Withdraw." A popup appeared: **"Your account is pending verification. To unlock withdrawals, you must deposit a one-time compliance deposit of ₦250,000."**

A message explained: "Due to rising regulatory standards, we require a compliance deposit to unlock your account for withdrawals. This is standard across all platforms and ensures legitimacy."

Tunde hesitated. But the profit was real on the screen. A ₦250,000 "fee" to unlock ₦500,000 seemed reasonable, even if strange.

He deposited ₦250,000.

### Step 6: The Trap Tightens

Withdrawal was still blocked. Now a new message: **"To comply with AML (Anti-Money Laundering) standards, we need to verify the source of your funds. Please provide proof."**

He uploaded screenshots of his bank account and a business registration. The app said: "Verification in progress."

A week later, a new message: **"Verification complete. To release your funds, a one-time 'tax clearance fee' of 15% must be paid. This ensures regulatory compliance."**

15% of ₦1.8 million was ₦270,000.

Tunde realized something was wrong. But the money was already in the system. He searched online for "TradeSmart withdrawal issues" and found Reddit posts from dozens of people in the same situation.

He did not pay. Neither did he recover his money. The app still shows ₦1.8 million in his account, but he cannot access it.

**Total loss: ₦1.75 million (~$1,100 USD).**

## Where the Scammer Won

<Warning>
- **Convincing UI/UX:** The app looked professional, worked smoothly, and showed consistent (if impossible) returns.
- **Regulatory language:** Fake "compliance fees," "AML verification," and "tax clearance" sound official.
- **Psychological sunk cost:** After the first withdrawal, the victim has already "invested" and is more willing to pay additional fees to recover funds.
- **Minimal KYC:** No real verification meant anyone could set it up.
</Warning>

---

## Red Flags (What Tunde Missed)

<Warning>
- [ ] **Daily returns of 2.5% = 912% annually.** No legitimate trading generates this reliably.
- [ ] **No clear company information** or regulatory license listed.
- [ ] **App is only available via APK or a single platform**, not on verified app stores with long history.
- [ ] **Cannot verify trades on public exchanges** (check blockchain or exchange APIs).
- [ ] **Withdrawal "compliance fees"** are a classic scam trap—legitimate platforms never block withdrawals for extra fees.
</Warning>

## If This Happens to You

<Steps>
  <Step title="Stop Depositing Immediately">
    Do not send any more funds.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Do Not Pay Any Fees">
    Do not pay any "compliance," "tax," or "verification" fees.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Do Not Provide Sensitive Documents">
    Do not provide ID, passport, or other sensitive documents.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Document Everything and Report">
    App name and version to Google Play / Apple App Store. Platform URL and screenshots to ChainAbuse and your local cybercrime unit. Bank account numbers used to your bank (they may flag similar transfers).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Action Steps

### Prevention

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Verify Licensing" icon="file-contract">
**Check:** Verify licensing on official regulator websites (Nigeria: SEC, CBN; Kenya: CMA; Ghana: SEC).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Cross-Check Returns Claims" icon="chart-line">
**Rule:** Cross-check returns claims against real market performance—2.5% daily is a red flag.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Perform a Withdrawal Test" icon="flask">
**Rule:** Perform a withdrawal test with a small amount before depositing large sums.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Check App Reviews" icon="star">
**Check:** Check app reviews on Reddit and crypto Twitter for "unable to withdraw" complaints.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Use Regulated Exchanges" icon="shield-check">
**Rule:** Use only regulated, well-known exchanges for trading (Binance, Kraken, Coinbase, etc.).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
